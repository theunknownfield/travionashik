Little bit about nashik

Nashik is known as one of the best cities in Maharashtra for its climate. Nashik has many beautiful pilgrims and a mythological history that attracts tourists from all over the world. It Is a city in the Northern region of the Indian state of Maharashtra situated on the banks of river Godavari. Nashik is the third largest city in Maharashtra after Mumbai and Pune. Earlier this city was known as Gulshanabad. There is a story about the name Nashik. Lord Shri Ram, Sita, and Laxman stayed in the city during their exile. Laxman the brother of lord Shri Ram cuts the nose of Shurphanakha who was the sister of Ravana, And thus this city is named Nasika to Nasik or Nashik. Some people in Nashik belong to different regions like Gujrat, Rajasthan, Dhule, Jalgaon, etc. Marathi is the main language that is mostly spoken in Nashik. The people of Nashik are very religious, and perform all the rituals and festivals with pride. They are known as Nashikkar. Nashik is also famous for its education after Pune as there are multiple top colleges situated in Nashik for higher education. Students from different places in Maharashtra came here for their higher studies. Kumbh Mela or Kumbha Mela is a major festival of Hinduism that comes after every 12 years in Nashik. Sadhus, Yogis, and Saints from all over India and the Himalayas come here to perform the rituals. Panchavati, Ramkund, Tapowan, Kalaram Temple, Trimbakeshwar, Swami Narayan, Sundar Narayan Mandir, Kapalehswar Mahadev, Vani(SASPTSRUNGI DEVI), Gangapur Dam, Pandav Leni, Someshwar are some of the most visited places in Nashik. Nashik is world-famous for grapes cultivation and exports of grapes to various countries. It is known as the Wine Capital of India. Nashik is one of the major exporters and providers of fresh vegetables, fruits, cereal grains, etc. all over India as well as outside India. The Gangapur dam built in Nashik is the first and largest soil dam in the world. The first currency factory in India was established in Nashik in 1981. This city has the best moments, climate and people. People fall in love with this city once they visit. Traveling to Nashik is easy or comfortable for outsiders. People can come to Nashik by Train, Car, and Air. Public transport like buses and auto rickshaw is best to travel inside the city.
